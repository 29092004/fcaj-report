> Here, I would like to share my personal opinions, experiences, and objective evaluations regarding my internship journey in the **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)** program at **AWS Vietnam**:

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The working environment at AWS Vietnam is highly professional, open, and encourages individual creativity. Interns are given the opportunity to work directly with modern cloud computing infrastructures and use industry-standard communication platforms such as Slack, Chime, and Git/Jira. The workspace (both remote and on-site) is collaborative and welcoming.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**  
I am deeply grateful for the guidance and constant support from my Mentor and the Program Administrators (Team Admin). Rather than simply giving answers, my mentor focused on teaching me a structured problem-solving methodology: encouraging me to read documentation, analyze application logs, and troubleshoot issues independently before providing feedback. The Team Admin supported all administrative requests swiftly, provided fully funded AWS practice accounts, and ensured a seamless learning environment.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**  
My assignments on the **Jobs-Matching-Platform** project align perfectly with my Computer Science/Information Technology major. Tasks such as building Next.js user interfaces, writing Infrastructure-as-Code (IaC) via AWS SAM templates, integrating DynamoDB and Cognito, and hosting the application on Amplify allowed me to apply theoretical database, networking, and software engineering concepts to a production-grade system.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
This internship has been a powerful launchpad for both my technical and professional growth. Technically, I built hands-on expertise in key AWS services including VPC, IAM, Lambda, API Gateway, DynamoDB, Cognito, S3, and Amplify. Professionally, I significantly improved my skills in reading technical English documentation, working in Agile/Scrum teams, and writing professional bilingual technical reports.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
The culture here promotes mutual respect, equality, and collaboration. There is no rigid hierarchy between interns and senior engineers, and everyone's input is valued constructively. When facing complex bugs or tight project deadlines, the team spirit shines through cross-team support, reducing pressure and optimizing the overall quality of the product.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**  
The program provides great benefits and support policies. The internship stipend allows us to focus entirely on learning. Moreover, free access to official AWS training resources and the opportunity to work on real AWS accounts are invaluable benefits that will benefit my future career path.

**7. Technical Tools and Infrastructure**  
The program provides AWS sandbox accounts with reasonable budget limits and proper IAM policies. This allows us to freely experiment without worrying about cloud infrastructure costs. The provided workshop templates and setup guides significantly minimized our initial setup overhead.

**8. Cross-team Collaboration and Networking**  
I highly appreciate the cross-learning opportunities organized between the different intern tracks (AI, Cloud, and Web). This helped us learn about integration, understanding how the AI track designed the API and how the Cloud track operated WAF to protect the API Gateway, providing a more comprehensive engineering perspective.

**9. Project Management and Coordination Process**  
The weekly progress tracking (Worklog) and periodic reviews train interns to build high self-discipline, manage time effectively, and establish the habit of proactively reporting work status, simulating a professional corporate environment.

### Additional Questions

* **What did you find most satisfying during your internship?**
  * Having the opportunity to design and deploy a real product (**Jobs-Matching-Platform**) from frontend Next.js pages to backend serverless resources. Learning how to identify root causes and approach technical problems systematically under the guidance of my Mentor was a transformative experience.
* **Evaluation of the training materials and learning roadmap of the program:**
  * The learning path is systematically designed from foundational concepts to advanced cloud patterns (from VPC, IAM to Serverless, SAM, and Amplify). Official AWS Documentation is rich and highly detailed. Although reading documentation entirely in technical English might present an initial hurdle, it greatly pushed me to enhance my self-research capabilities and professional English proficiency.
* **Job readiness for real-world projects after completing the internship:**
  * I feel around 85% more confident when approaching system design challenges for Cloud and Serverless architectures. Having hands-on experience solving connection issues, IAM policies, S3 CORS configurations, and API Gateway JWT Authorizers means I am no longer intimidated by production systems.
* **Future plans for continuous self-learning and professional development:**
  * My short-term goal for the next 3 to 6 months is to solidify the knowledge I acquired, refine the Jobs-Matching-Platform application, and prepare for the **AWS Certified Developer – Associate** certification exam to formally standardize my professional capabilities on the job market.
* **What do you think the company should improve for future interns?**
  * The program could build a more detailed and visual Onboarding Checklist covering the serverless architecture of the project in the first week. This would help subsequent cohorts get up to speed faster and understand the initial SAM template structure more easily.
* **If recommending to a friend, would you suggest they intern here? Why or why not?**
  * Absolutely. I would strongly recommend this program to fellow IT students because it offers a genuine hands-on learning experience where interns are allowed to try, fail, learn, and receive clear career-development direction.

  * I suggest hosting brief cross-project presentations or tech-sharing sessions among different teams (AI, Cloud, Web/Mobile) so interns can exchange knowledge and gain exposure to different engineering perspectives.
* **Would you like to continue this program in the future?**
  * I hope to have the opportunity to continue contributing to this program in other roles or have the opportunity to work in a permanent engineering position at AWS Vietnam in the future.
* **Any other comments (free sharing):**
  * I would like to express my heartfelt gratitude to the First Cloud AI Journey organizers and mentors for dedicating their time and effort to support, guide, and teach us throughout this highly rewarding internship.
