# Summary Report: "FCAJ Tech Sharing & Demo Day - 23/05/2026"

### Event Objectives

- Share real-world projects and advanced technology solutions developed by community members.
- Introduce in-depth knowledge on Generative AI, Large Language Models (LLM), and Multi-Agent Systems.
- Promote optimization solutions for cloud infrastructure using AWS CloudFront.
- Share practical experiences from competing in large-scale technology Hackathons.

### Speakers & Topics

- **Anh Tịnh** - Topic: *Build Second Brain*
- **Hải Anh** - Topic: *Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick*
- **Thịnh** - Topic: *From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation*
- **Team VIB** - Topic: *36 hrs with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality*
- **Đào Đức** - Topic: *Deep dive talk: How LLM actually works?*
- **Cát Vy** - Topic: *Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System: The Case of Startup Credit Scoring*

### Key Highlights from Presentations

#### 1. Build Second Brain - Speaker: Anh Tịnh
- Introduced the Personal Knowledge Management (PKM) methodology in the digital era.
- How to structure information storage and connect thoughts to boost study and work productivity.

#### 2. Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick - Speaker: Hải Anh
- Demonstrated building a smart chatbot to support user requests utilizing AWS cloud infrastructure.
- Leveraged AI tools for rapid and user-friendly visual data analysis.

#### 3. CloudFront Optimization (From Edge To Origin) - Speaker: Thịnh
- Analyzed the mechanics of Amazon CloudFront CDN to accelerate resource delivery.
- Configured routes from Edge Locations back to Origin Servers to minimize latency and improve system security.

#### 4. 36 Hours at LotusHacks Hackathon - Team VIB
- Shared practical experiences in developing the UTMorpho application from a raw concept to a working product under tight time constraints.
- Discussed team collaboration, task delegation, and rapid troubleshooting during the event.

#### 5. Under the Hood of Large Language Models (LLM) - Speaker: Đào Đức
- Deep dived into Transformer architectures and Self-Attention mechanisms that enable LLMs to understand and generate natural language.
- Explained the Pre-training and Fine-tuning processes utilized to optimize models for specialized business tasks.

#### 6. Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent Systems - Speaker: Cát Vy
- Introduced Multi-Agent System designs to automate complex corporate workflows.
- Real-world case study: Applying Multi-Agent systems to automated financial analysis and credit scoring for startups.

### Key Takeaways

#### On Generative AI & Emerging Technologies
- Gained a clear understanding of the mathematical and engineering details behind how LLMs process tokenized text.
- Learned about Multi-Agent architectures that automate complex, multi-step enterprise workflows.

#### On Cloud Infrastructure
- Mastered global latency optimization using Amazon CloudFront.
- Understood how to integrate AWS AI services into functional application frontends.

#### On Soft Skills & Teamwork
- Learned personal knowledge management methods to enhance self-learning efficiency.
- Acquired insights on managing stress and collaborative coding from Team VIB's Hackathon case study.

### Applying to Work

- **Build Second Brain:** Apply systematic note-taking and knowledge mapping to organize technical documentation during my internship.
- **CDN Optimization:** Explore Amazon CloudFront configurations for personal web projects to reduce data egress fees.
- **GenAI Research:** Experiment with LLMs and construct simple AI agents to automate repetitive developer workflows.
- **Rapid Prototyping:** Apply Hackathon lessons to streamline coding processes, focusing heavily on building a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) first.

### Event Experience

Attending this tech sharing session was highly practical and inspiring:
- Gained direct exposure to actual project demos rather than abstract theoretical slide decks.
- Witnessed active, open panel discussions where speakers answered technical questions in detail.
- Gathered invaluable "war stories" regarding both code implementation and time/team management.

#### Lessons Learned
- Technologies evolve extremely rapidly, especially in GenAI, requiring strong personal self-learning and knowledge management practices.
- System designs must always address concrete business problems (such as the startup credit scoring case study).
- Perseverance and high-pressure team collaboration are critical keys to bringing creative ideas to reality.
